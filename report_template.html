🎯 Conclusion

March 2025 demonstrated exceptional performance across all operational categories for the robotic cleaning pilot program at Marston Science Library and Dental Science Building. The system exceeded efficiency and cost-effectiveness targets while maintaining consistent service quality throughout both facilities.

The pilot deployment achieved 97.8% system availability, 96.3% task completion rate, and $12,400 in monthly cost savings compared to traditional cleaning methods. These results validate the strategic investment in robotic cleaning technology and support expansion planning for additional campus facilities.

Continuous monitoring and optimization have resulted in measurable improvements in energy efficiency, coverage completeness, and operational reliability. The system's successful adaptation to diverse facility requirements—from quiet library operations to clinical environment sanitization—demonstrates the technology's versatility and effectiveness for higher education applications.
